## (A) Virus-capsid databases / T-numbers — VIROCAPSID C3b

- **VIPERdb v3.0** (2021) — Montiel-Garcia D. et al., "VIPERdb v3.0: a structure-based data analytics platform for viral capsids," *Nucleic Acids Research* 49(D1):D809–D816, DOI 10.1093/nar/gkaa1096, PMID 33313778, PMC7779063. Earlier: VIPERdb2 (Carrillo-Tripp et al., *NAR* 2009, PMC2686430); original VIPER (Reddy et al., *J. Virol.* 2001, PMC116089). **Scale: 931 capsid structures across 62 virus families** (non-enveloped icosahedral, placed in a common "VIPER convention"). **T-number is a first-class annotation** (entries record diameter, architecture = T-number, genome type, taxonomy, intersubunit energies, surface residues); there is a sortable **"T-number Index" / family index** (Top Menu → Data) and per-family T charts. **Web API (WAPI)**: HTTP/PHP endpoints (`viperdb.org` / `viperdb.scripps.edu/Developers_guide.php`), output **CSV / HTML / XML / JSON / PDB / FASTA**. So an n ≥ 100 (in fact ~800) (PDB-ID, T, family) corpus is directly obtainable. (The `.org`/`.scripps.edu` TLS certs are flaky to automated fetchers but fine in a browser; canonical paper at https://academic.oup.com/nar/article/49/D1/D809/6031427 / https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7779063/.)
- **RCSB PDB**: annotates per-assembly **point-group symmetry** ("Global Symmetry: Icosahedral – I") + **stoichiometry** (e.g. "Hetero 240-mer – A60B60C60D60") and exposes a **Protein Symmetry Browser** (`rcsb.org/search/browse/symmetry`) + Search-API field `rcsb_struct_symmetry` — but **T-number is not a queryable field**. You can coarsely derive T from copy number (60→T1, 180→T3, 240→T4, 420→T7, …) but VIPERdb already does this carefully (pseudo-T, ASU-vs-full-particle subtleties) — use its curated value. Docs: https://www.rcsb.org/docs/general-help/symmetry-resources-in-the-pdb.
- **EMDB**: annotates point-group symmetry in metadata (~20% of holdings are viruses, mostly icosahedrally averaged) but **not** Caspar–Klug T-number.
- **Reliability**: VIPERdb T-numbers are curator-assigned from the icosahedral reconstruction, generally reliable for classical CK capsids; caveats for non-quasi-equivalent / exceptional capsids (papovavirus all-pentamer T=7d, Twarock–Luque Archimedean-lattice capsids, prolate phage heads) and for entries that are only an ASU. For a "σ(6)=12 ⇒ always 12 pentamers ∀T" audit this is exactly the right corpus — CK geometry *forces* 12 pentamers (60 5-fold-axis subunits) for every icosahedral T. Geometry refs: Caspar & Klug 1962 (*Cold Spring Harb. Symp. Quant. Biol.* 27:1–24); Twarock & Luque, *Nat. Commun.* 10:4414 (2019) "Structural puzzles in virology solved with an overarching icosahedral design principle"; Luque & Reguera, *The Biochemist* 43(1):20 (2021) "Models of viral capsid symmetry as a driver of discovery"; PDB-101 Molecule-of-the-Month #200 "Quasisymmetry in Icosahedral Viruses" (https://pdb101.rcsb.org/motm/200).

## (B) RNA / antisense off-target screening — RIBOZYME G26-RB-3

- **Reference transcriptomes**: GENCODE v47 (human, GRCh38, Oct 2024) all-transcript FASTA `gencode.v47.transcripts.fa.gz` at `https://ftp.ebi.ac.uk/pub/databases/gencode/Gencode_human/release_47/gencode.v47.transcripts.fa.gz` (protein-coding-only: `gencode.v47.pc_transcripts.fa.gz`; ~250k transcripts / ~62k genes); release index https://www.gencodegenes.org/human/release_47.html; GENCODE 2025 paper *NAR* 53(D1):D966. Alternatives: RefSeq human RNA `GCF_000001405.40_GRCh38.p14_rna.fna.gz` (NCBI); Ensembl `Homo_sapiens.GRCh38.cdna.all.fa.gz`.
- **Tools** (the ones actually used for short-oligo-vs-transcriptome screens):
  - **RIsearch2** — *the* standard for siRNA/short-RNA off-target prediction at transcriptome scale (suffix-array seed-and-extend; near-complementary RNA–RNA interactions transcriptome-wide; has an "off-targeting potential" pipeline combining hits with target-site accessibility + transcript abundance). Alkan F. et al., *NAR* 45(8):e60 (2017), PMID 28108657. Software: https://rth.dk/resources/risearch/ (stand-alone C). **Closest match to "screen a short antisense arm against a whole transcriptome with biophysically meaningful scoring."**
  - **OligoWalk / Sfold** (Mathews/Ding) — accessibility + hybridization thermodynamics; **siDirect, siSPOTR, RNAxs** — siRNA design with seed-based off-target filters; **BLASTn-against-transcriptome** — the "is this oligo unique?" workhorse (used by ASOG, *CSBJ* 2025; ASOptimizer, *NAR* W1 2025; MASON web server, *NAR* 2023 W1, for bacterial-mRNA PNAs/PMOs); **bowtie/bowtie2/BWA** against the transcriptome FASTA — fast near-exact short-k-mer alignment; **Cas-OFFinder** (Bae/Park/Kim, *Bioinformatics* 30:1473, 2014; http://www.rgenome.net/cas-offinder/, GitHub snugel/cas-offinder) — genome-wide, *no mismatch-count limit*, bulges, arbitrary pattern length, OpenCL-accelerated; built for CRISPR guides but a general "find all sites within k mismatches of this short pattern in this FASTA" engine.
  - Reviews/benchmarks: Bereczki et al., "Mitigating off-target effects of small RNAs…," *Br. J. Pharmacol.* (2025); Damle et al., "A Workflow for Transcriptome-Wide Assessment of Antisense Oligonucleotide Selectivity," *Nucleic Acid Ther.* 35(6):249 (2025), PMID 41021289 — argues in-silico rules are still suboptimal, pushes experimental concentration-response 3′Tag-Seq; *takeaway*: even a single mismatch can give significant knockdown ⇒ a credible in-silico screen must be stringent (count ≤1–2 mismatch sites, weight by accessibility + abundance, treat G·U wobble as near-match).
- **Hammerhead specifics**: trans-acting hammerhead cleaves 3′ of an **NUH** triplet → revised **NHH** rule (NUH ∪ NCH ∪ some NAH; Zoumadakis & Tabler, *NAR* 23:1192, 1995; Kore et al., *NAR* 26:4116, 1998). Stems I & III (the substrate-recognition arms) are simply Watson–Crick complementary to the flanks of that triplet. Specificity/turnover trade-off: ~6–8 bp arms turn over well but discriminate poorly (HH16, Werner & Uhlenbeck, *NAR* 23:2092, 1995); for structured mRNA the practical optimum is **>10 nt per arm**. No siRNA-style "seed" with a sharp boundary — the screen should look for *complementarity blocks* (≥ ~10–12 contiguous, wobble-tolerant, adjacent to an NHH triplet), not fixed-k exact matches. (PMC4770207 "Automated design of hammerhead ribozymes…PABPN1"; PMC12192606 review.)

## (C) Lean 4 / mathlib & Π¹₁-CA₀ — QUANTUM GATE-26-2

- **mathlib4 has**: `Fintype.card_perm : Fintype.card (Equiv.Perm α) = (Fintype.card α)!` (`Mathlib/Data/Fintype/Perm.lean`) ⇒ `Fintype.card (Equiv.Perm (Fin 4)) = 4! = 24` (by `decide`/`simp [Fintype.card_perm]`); `alternatingGroup` (`Mathlib/GroupTheory/SpecificGroups/Alternating.lean`); `Nat.totient` + `Nat.sum_totient` (`Mathlib/Data/Nat/Totient.lean`); `Nat.ArithmeticFunction.sigma` (`Mathlib/NumberTheory/ArithmeticFunction.lean`) — `σ k n = ∑ d ∈ n.divisors, d^k`, so `σ 1 6 = 1+2+3+6 = 12` is `decide`; `DihedralGroup`, `QuaternionGroup` (`Mathlib/GroupTheory/SpecificGroups/`); Sylow theory; `alternatingGroup (Fin 5)` simplicity; classification of finite abelian groups (`Mathlib/GroupTheory/FiniteAbelian/Basic.lean`). **mathlib does NOT have**: "rotation group of the cube ≅ S₄" (not packaged — but a few-hundred-line exercise: cube's 4 space-diagonals → faithful transitive action ⇒ embeds in S₄, order 24 ⇒ iso; or an explicit `MulEquiv` checked by `decide`); "V−E+F=2 for convex polyhedra" (not in mathlib; formalized in **Mizar** — Pąk, "Euler's Polyhedron Formula," *Formalized Mathematics* 2008 — and in **HOL Light** ("Top 100 theorems" tracker); a real but well-trodden porting task); **no** formalization of Caspar–Klug quasi-equivalence / "12 pentamers" in *any* proof assistant (would be a first; reduces to combinatorics of the hexagonal lattice mod the icosahedral group — "5 valence-5 vertices ⇒ exactly 12 pentamers, 10(T−1) hexamers" is elementary discrete geometry).
- **Π¹₁-CA₀** = the subsystem of second-order arithmetic with **Π¹₁-comprehension** (for any Π¹₁ φ(n), {n : φ(n)} exists). Strongest of Simpson's "Big Five" (RCA₀ ⊂ WKL₀ ⊂ ACA₀ ⊂ ATR₀ ⊂ Π¹₁-CA₀), **fully impredicative**, proof-theoretic ordinal Ψ₀(Ω_ω); calibrated by infinitary statements (Cantor–Bendixson, "every countable abelian group = divisible ⊕ reduced," Silver's dichotomy, …). Ref: S.G. Simpson, *Subsystems of Second Order Arithmetic*, 2nd ed., Cambridge UP / ASL (2009) — Ch. I overview (https://sgslogic.net/t20/sosoa/chapter1.pdf), Ch. VI (Π¹₁-CA₀); SEP "Reverse Mathematics" (plato.stanford.edu/entries/reverse-mathematics/). **Why it's overkill for the n=6-lattice claims**: \|S₄\|=24, \|O\|=24, S₄≅O, χ=2 for a *given* polyhedron, "12 pentamers for a *given* T", the Eyring TST rate identity, the master-identity arithmetic — all **Δ⁰₀/decidable** (or, for Eyring, an algebraic identity over ℝ provable in RCA₀ with the standard analysis library). Provable in **PRA** (primitive recursive arithmetic = finitism, Tait) and *a fortiori* in **RCA₀**; in Lean: `by decide` / `by norm_num` / `by simp`. Citing Π¹₁-CA₀ for "4! = 24" ≈ citing Hahn–Banach for "2+2=4". Honest target: **"a Lean 4 `decide`/`Decidable`-backed certificate (formal strength ≤ RCA₀ ≈ PRA)"** + an explicit note that the prior "Π¹₁-CA₀" label was a mis-calibration.
- Context (what's tractable in formalized finite group theory): Gonthier et al., "A Machine-Checked Proof of the Odd Order Theorem" (Feit–Thompson), ITP 2013 — ~150k lines Coq/Rocq + the SSReflect/MathComp finite-group libraries (largest finite-group formalization to date); CFSG itself "far out of reach" but a 2nd-gen-proof formalization "nearing completion." Smaller: Harper & Wu, "Classifying the groups of order pq in Lean," arXiv:2501.09769 (2025).

## (E) VQE for drug-target binding ranking + Mpro/nirmatrelvir mechanism — QUANTUM Phase D

- **State of the art (2023–2026)**: people *do* "compute a VQE-in-a-small-active-space energy for a drug-relevant fragment/reaction and use it to rank/refine," but always (a) tiny active spaces (≈2–20 qubits on simulators; ≈single-digit qubits with meaningful results on real hardware), (b) embedded as a QM correction inside a classical QM/MM or ML pipeline (never a standalone affinity engine), (c) *without* demonstrated quantum advantage over CASCI/CASSCF on the same active space. The existing Mpro/nirmatrelvir (2e,2o)→2-qubit, sub-µHa-vs-CASCI(2,2) demo is squarely in the genre.
  - **Li et al., "A hybrid quantum computing pipeline for real world drug discovery," arXiv:2401.03759 / *Sci. Rep.* 14 (2024) 16953** — VQE inside a QM/MM workflow on a **covalent KRAS-G12C inhibitor**; HF/CASCI/VQE agree closely because the active space is small; VQE = the wavefunction engine for "medium-sized" fragments. (Closest published analogue; also targets KRAS-G12C.)
  - **IBM–Merck (Mensa et al.)** — VQE for **H-bond interaction energies in small-molecule kinase inhibitors**, accuracy "comparable to DFT," no speedup.
  - **Lau et al., "Quantum Computing-Enhanced Algorithm Unveils Novel Inhibitors for KRAS," arXiv:2402.08210 / *Nat. Biotechnol.* (2024)** — 16-qubit quantum-classical *generative* model for KRAS inhibitors, 15 compounds synthesized & tested (generative, not energy-ranking — useful contrast).
  - **Survey: Pyrkov et al., "Quantum-machine-assisted Drug Discovery: Survey and Perspective," arXiv:2408.13479** — for VQE: "small active spaces, hybrid embedding, near-term hardware not yet competitive."
  - **Active-space-VQE-for-drug-molecules benchmark (2025–26, e.g. arXiv:2512.18203)** — chemically-motivated active spaces for ~7 representative drug molecules; UCCSD-VQE + hardware-efficient VQE on simulators & QPUs; scores chemistry (energy error vs FCI/CASCI) + architecture (qubit count, depth) — the rubric for a "5–10 candidate library ranking via VQE."
  - **Scale reference**: ~50-orbital active spaces "in ~days on a *fault-tolerant* machine" (e.g. arXiv:2501.06165 "Faster quantum chemistry simulations…") = a future-FTQC projection, not NISQ reality. Simulators today: exact-statevector VQE comfortable to ~20–24 qubits (~10–12 spatial orbitals); real hardware: "chemically meaningful" VQE still essentially ≤4–6 qubits.
- **Mpro / nirmatrelvir mechanism (confirmed, with refs)**: SARS-CoV-2 Mpro (= 3CLpro, nsp5) is a **cysteine protease with a Cys145–His41 catalytic dyad** — His41 = general base that **deprotonates Cys145 to the thiolate**; the thiolate = nucleophile. Substrate catalysis: Cys145-Sγ attacks the scissile (P1=Gln) carbonyl → tetrahedral **thiohemiketal**, developing oxyanion held in the **oxyanion hole = backbone amide NHs of Gly143, Ser144, Cys145**; His41 protonates the leaving amine; acyl-enzyme hydrolysis regenerates the enzyme. **Nirmatrelvir (PF-07321332; the Mpro inhibitor in Paxlovid) = covalent-*reversible* nitrile warhead**: Cys145 thiolate (His41-deprotonated) makes nucleophilic attack on the **nitrile carbon (–C≡N) → reversible covalent thioimidate adduct (–S–C(=NH)–)**, the imino/charge again held in the Gly143/Ser144/Cys145 oxyanion hole. **S1 pocket / P1 specificity**: Mpro is essentially absolutely Gln-specific at P1; **S1 lined by Phe140, Leu141, Asn142, His163, Glu166, His172** (His163 H-bonds the P1 side-chain carbonyl, Glu166 caps); nirmatrelvir uses an **(S)-γ-lactam (2-oxopyrrolidin-3-yl) glutamine surrogate at P1**, plus a 6,6-dimethyl-3-azabicyclo[3.1.0]hexane Leu-mimic at P2 (into S2) and a trifluoroacetyl P3/P4 cap (metabolic-stability handle). Refs: **Owen et al., "An oral SARS-CoV-2 Mpro inhibitor clinical candidate…," *Science* 374:1586–1593 (2021)**, DOI 10.1126/science.abl4784, PMID 34726479 (the canonical nirmatrelvir paper); **Zhang et al., "Crystal structure of SARS-CoV-2 main protease…," *Science* 368:409–412 (2020)**, DOI 10.1126/science.abb3405 (PDB 6Y2E/6Y2F/6Y2G); **Jin et al., "Structure of Mpro from SARS-CoV-2 and discovery of its inhibitors," *Nature* 582:289–293 (2020)** (PDB 6LU7 + N3); QM/MM mechanism — **Ramos-Guzmán et al., "Multiscale Simulations of the Covalent Inhibition of the SARS-CoV-2 Main Protease: Four Compounds and Three Reaction Mechanisms," *JACS* 145 (2023)**, DOI 10.1021/jacs.3c02229; *J. Med. Chem.* "Fixing the Achilles Heel of Paxlovid," 67 (2024).
- **Practical conclusion for F-Q-6-F**: a "5–10 candidate library ranking via VQE" for an Mpro covalent-warhead series is methodologically in-line with the literature *iff* (i) each candidate's active space is minimal (the Cys-Sγ / warhead-π* / His41 region — the (2,2)→2-qubit scheme, optionally (4,4)→8-qubit), (ii) compute *relative* reaction/binding energy (ΔΔE between warheads, rest of the system classical — QM/MM or gas-phase fragment ΔE), (iii) validate every VQE number against CASCI on the same space (as already done), (iv) report as "VQE reproduces CASCI to sub-µHa; the ranking is a CASCI ranking with a quantum-algorithm wrapper" — not a quantum-advantage claim.
